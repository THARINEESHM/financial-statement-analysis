# Financial Statement Analysis — Infosys Limited (FY2022–FY2024)

## Objective
Analyze 3 years of Infosys Limited's standalone financial statements 
to evaluate liquidity, profitability, and leverage trends using 
standard accounting ratios.

## Company Analyzed
**Infosys Limited (NSE: INFY)**  
India's second-largest IT services company.  
Period: FY2022, FY2023, FY2024  
Data Source: Standalone Financial Statements from official Infosys Annual Reports

## Ratios Calculated (11 Total)

**Liquidity:** Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Cash Ratio  
**Profitability:** Gross Profit Margin, Operating Margin, Net Profit Margin, ROA, ROE  
**Leverage:** Debt to Equity, Debt Ratio, Interest Coverage Ratio

## Key Findings
1.  **Liquidity Improvement:** Current Ratio rose from 1.90x (FY2023) to 2.43x (FY2025), and cash holdings nearly doubled to ₹14,265 Cr, reflecting strong free cash flow generation.
2. **Gross Margin Expansion:** Gross Profit Margin improved from 34.0% to 36.4% over 3 years, indicating improving service delivery efficiency and cost discipline on core operations.
3. **Net Margin Pressure in FY2025:**	Net Profit Margin declined from 21.1% (FY2024) to 18.7% (FY2025) as software package and AI infrastructure costs surged, compressing the operating line.
4. **Strong Shareholder Returns:**	Return on Equity remained above 29% in FY2025 despite pressure, and ROA of 20.5% reflects efficient asset utilization across the 3-year period.
5. **Debt-Free Capital Structure:**	Zero borrowings across all 3 years — Infosys is entirely equity-financed, confirming minimal financial risk and conservative capital management.
